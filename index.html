Jonas Edward Salk

The man who changed the course of history

Timeline of Jonas Salk's life:
  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1934 - Graduated from Townsend Harris High School, a school for gifted students.
  • 1939 - Received his M.D. from New York University School of Medicine.
  • 1942 - Began working at the University of Michigan, where he worked on developing flu vaccines.
  • 1955 - The Salk polio vaccine was declared safe and effective, leading to widespread vaccination and a dramatic reduction in polio cases worldwide.
  • 1963 - Founded the Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla, California, which became a leading center for medical and scientific research.
  • 1977 - Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Jimmy Carter.
  • 1995 - Jonas Salk passed away in La Jolla, California, leaving behind a legacy of scientific achievement and humanitarian impact.
